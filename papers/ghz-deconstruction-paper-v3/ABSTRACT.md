# ABSTRACT: The Geometric Closure of GHZ Correlations

**GHZ Deconstruction: Version 3 (The Three-Stones Audit)**

The Greenberger-Horne-Zeilinger (GHZ) effect is traditionally presented as the strongest evidence for non-local entanglement, as it predicts a contradiction with local realism that does not rely on inequalities, but on perfect correlations (F=4). This paper presents a complete deconstruction of this claim using a local "Ripple Model" based on three emitters in a geometric loop.

We demonstrate that:
1. **Geometric Phase-Loop**: The 3-body correlation is an identity of a closed-loop interference system where the sum of relative phases is zero. This system is strictly local.
2. **The 2.828 Signature**: Using Normalized Cross-Correlation (NCC), the local ripple model produces a Mermin F-value of $2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.828$, significantly exceeding the binarized LHV limit.
3. **The Binarization Crush**: We prove that forcing wave-interference outcomes into binary +/-1 logic "crushes" the F-value exactly to 2.0. This confirms that the Bell/Mermin limit is a property of the sampling protocol, not the physical world.

This audit sit the case that the GHZ violation is a geometric artifact of wave-normalization, providing a clear path to understanding quantum correlations without recourse to non-locality.
