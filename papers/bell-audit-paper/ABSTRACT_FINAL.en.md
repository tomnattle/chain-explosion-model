# Bell External Abstract (English)

We present a denominator-audit framework for Bell/CHSH statistics to separate mechanism effects from protocol-bookkeeping effects under a shared data context. The study evaluates strict and standard protocols in parallel and explicitly separates decision criteria into an engineering gate (computability and data sufficiency) and a thesis gate (preregistered claim constraints), preventing direct promotion of runnable outputs into publication-level claims. On the same NIST completeblind event stream, binary CHSH gives `S_strict=2.336276` (window `0.0`) and `S_standard=2.839387` (window `15.0`), a rule-driven shift of `+0.503111`. Bootstrap (`n=2000`) yields `95% CI_strict=[2.295151, 2.378669]` and `95% CI_standard=[2.820420, 2.857413]`; the standard CI includes `2sqrt(2)=2.828427`, so we do not claim a Tsirelson violation. A simulated fallback configuration further shows that `S_standard` does not necessarily exceed `S_strict`, reinforcing protocol sensitivity to pairing and inclusion rules. We therefore report a method-level conclusion: Bell/CHSH outcomes are highly sensitive to declared analysis assumptions, and strong claims require joint reporting of protocol definitions, uncertainty intervals, and retained negative results.
