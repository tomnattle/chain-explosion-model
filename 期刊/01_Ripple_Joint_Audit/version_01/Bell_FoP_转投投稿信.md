Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript entitled **“Denominator-Audit Robustness Analysis for Bell/CHSH Statistics: A Reconciliation Study of Strict vs Standard Protocols”** for publication in *Foundations of Physics*.

The manuscript addresses a foundational question through a methodological lens: to what extent can Bell/CHSH-level interpretive claims depend on protocol definitions applied to the same event stream. Rather than advancing a new ontology, we perform a reproducible audit of estimator construction and pairing rules under shared data conditions.

Using the NIST completeblind dataset, we compare strict and standard CHSH pipelines in parallel. On identical data, we obtain `S_strict = 2.336276` (window 0.0) and `S_standard = 2.839387` (window 15.0), i.e., `ΔS = +0.503111` induced by protocol choice alone. We further report bootstrap uncertainty intervals (`n=2000`): `CI95_strict = [2.295151, 2.378669]` and `CI95_standard = [2.820420, 2.857413]`. Since `2√2 = 2.828427` lies inside the standard interval, we explicitly do **not** claim a Tsirelson violation.

Our intended contribution is therefore boundary-aware and foundationally relevant: Bell/CHSH conclusions should be interpreted jointly with protocol definitions, retained negative results, and uncertainty intervals, instead of relying on point estimates alone. We hope this paper is of interest to *Foundations of Physics* readers concerned with the relation between statistical procedures and foundational interpretation.
